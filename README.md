## 🇬🇧 English

### Overview
This repository contains the computational model and analysis code developed for the **Fission Plant** course at **Politecnico di Torino**. 

The project focuses on a comprehensive, high-fidelity structural integrity analysis for the fuel cladding and $UO_2$ pellets of the **Westinghouse AP1000** Advanced Passive Pressurized Water Reactor (PWR). Operating under extreme in-core conditions—characterized by elevated temperature gradients, neutron irradiation, intense mechanical loads, and coolant pressure—requires rigorous and conservative structural verification in compliance with international nuclear codes, specifically the **ASME Boiler and Pressure Vessel Code (BPVC)**.

### Key Objectives & Analysis Features
- **Thermal-Mechanical Stress Evaluation:** Computes stress, strain, and displacement fields across the pellet-cladding radial cross-section during steady-state and transient operations.
- **ASME Code Compliance:** Evaluates primary and secondary stress limits, fatigue life usage, thermal creep, and buckling/collapse risks according to ASME Section III design criteria.
- **Pellet-Cladding Interaction (PCI/PCMI):** Models radial heat transfer, thermal expansion gaps, and mechanical contact forces under severe thermal gradients.
- **Conservative Margin Verification:** Incorporates conservative safety factors to account for material degradation, swelling, and irradiation-induced phenomena.

### Methodology & Standards
1. **Core Geometry & Operating Parameters:** Derived from standard AP1000 core data ($17 \times 17$ fuel assembly, Zircaloy-4 / ZIRLO cladding material properties, fuel burnup profiles).
2. **Stress Categorization:** 
   - *Primary Membrane Stress ($P_m$)*: Sustained internal gas pressure and external coolant pressure.
   - *Primary Bending Stress ($P_b$)*: Structural bending moments across fuel rod geometry.
   - *Secondary Thermal Stress ($Q$)*: Nonlinear radial temperature profiles generated by nuclear heat production.
3. **Failure Criteria:** Verification against ASME allowable stress limits ($S_m$), primary plus secondary stress intensity ranges ($3S_m$), cumulative usage factor (CUF) for fatigue, and creep limits.
